Introduction

Hi, I'm Chia-Chun Hung, a business analytics professional currently pursuing my master's degree at USC Marshall School of Business.

Early in my career

Early in my career, I started my analytics journey at an e-commerce company in Taiwan, where I helped secure more government contracts, analyzed customer segments and competitors, and optimized our pricing strategy.

Taking on harder challenges

As I grew, I took on harder challenges. I noticed our team was struggling with inefficiencies—we spent days manually collecting data for tender analysis, and our inventory was either running out or piling up in warehouses.

  • Automation: I taught myself Python and built a web crawler that automated bid research.
  • Forecasting: I developed a demand forecasting model that cut warehouse costs by 5%.

Hitting a ceiling

But then I hit a ceiling. I could handle structured data well, but I couldn't make sense of unstructured customer reviews that were causing our margin problems. I was solving problems as they came up instead of thinking ahead about what our data strategy should be.

And honestly, I wasn't comfortable networking in unstructured settings. I realized I needed more than just better tools—I needed to think differently.

That’s what brought me to USC Marshall

To become a better strategist, leader, and collaborator—and to find roles where I can use both technical skills and business thinking to build solutions that actually help companies grow.

If you want the short version: I’m most interested in work that connects strategy to execution—clear questions, measurable bets, and systems that help teams decide faster.
